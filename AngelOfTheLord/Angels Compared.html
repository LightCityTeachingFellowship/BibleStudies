Authority over the Two Witnesses
  • Certainly (It goes without saying)
  • Being the One through whom God commissioned and empowered Moses
    • He certainly has authority over Moses,
    • And if Moses, then over Elijah as well
  • No
  • He is apparently not Daniel’s Lord, and if he wasn’t Daniel’s Lord, then he certainly is not Moses’ and Elijah’s Lord
  • The two witnesses—Moses and Elijah—are his servants
    • He spoke of the two witnesses as his witnesses—“my two witnesses” [Rev 11:3]
  • The two witnesses—Moses and Elijah—are his servants
    • He is called their Lord [Rev 11:8]

Authority to Determine World Events
  • Certainly (It goes without saying)
  • He had power over prophetic events. If he did not, what was the point in swearing about the timing of the events, promising that there would be no more delay [Rev 10:5-7]
  • He has power over prophetic events to determine what will be and when—“Heaven and earth will pass… but my words will never pass away” [Mat 24:]
